Exploratory Data Analysis

Dataset Overview

  • Training set: 800 observations, 9 variables
  • Test set: 230 observations (ccs = NA — to predict)
  • Response: ccs — Concrete Compressive Strength (MPa)
  • Covariates: cement, slag, ash, water, superplasticizer, coarse_agg, fine_agg, age

Note

No missing values in training data. slag, ash, and superplasticizer have many zeros (formulations without those ingredients).

Response Distribution

Shapiro-Wilk: Raw ccs (W = 0.981) is closer to Normal than log(ccs) (W = 0.950). Log transformation overcorrects → introduces left-skew. We use raw ccs.

Scatterplots: Covariates vs CCS

Key observation: age shows a curved (diminishing-returns) relationship → motivates log(age) transformation.

Modeling Approach

Modeling Assumptions

  1. Linearity — Supported by scatterplots for most covariates
  2. Independence — Each observation is a separate mixture
  3. Normality of errors — Raw ccs is reasonably symmetric (skewness = +0.41)
  4. Homoscedasticity — Chose raw ccs over log(ccs) to maintain stable variance
  5. No perfect multicollinearity — VIFs elevated but manageable; priors regularize

Proposed Model

Likelihood: \[y_i \mid \boldsymbol{\beta}, \sigma^2 \sim N(\alpha + \mathbf{x}_i^\top \boldsymbol{\beta},\; \sigma^2)\]

Priors:

Parameter Prior
Intercept \(\alpha\) \(N(0, 10)\)
Slopes \(\beta_j\) \(N(0, 5)\)
Residual SD \(\sigma\) Half-Cauchy\((0, 5)\)

Covariates are standardized (mean 0, sd 1) before fitting.

Plan: Test prior sensitivity (tight/wide) + model formulations (interaction, log-age) via LOO-CV.

Prior Sensitivity Results

We tested three prior widths: Tight N(0,1), Base N(0,5), Wide N(0,50)

LOO-CV Comparison:

Wide (N(0,50)) 0.0 0.0

Tight (N(0,1)) −26.8 9.4
  • Base ≈ Wide → data dominates, priors don’t matter ✅
  • Tight is too restrictive → hurts predictions ❌
  • Base priors are well-calibrated

Important

The log-age model wins by 300+ elpd — an overwhelming improvement. The log(age) transformation captures the nonlinear curing curve that was visible in the EDA scatterplots.

Key Findings

  • cement and age (log-transformed) are the strongest predictors — their 95% credible intervals are firmly away from zero
  • water has a strong negative effect on strength
  • slag and ash are also significant positive predictors
  • coarse_agg and fine_agg are not significant — intervals include zero
  • The log-age model was selected as the best formulation via LOO-CV (+300 elpd over base)
  • Prior sensitivity confirmed results are data-driven, not prior-driven
